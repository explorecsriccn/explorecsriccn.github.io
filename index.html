Welcome

The Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee (IIT Roorkee), India, is thrilled to partner with Google Research to offer the ExploreCSR program, providing a valuable research experience for bachelor's and master's students. We wholeheartedly encourage applications from students belonging to historically underrepresented groups, women, remotely located college students, and those from low socioeconomic backgrounds. Our main goal is to introduce students to the diverse aspects of computing research by engaging with expert faculty from various top-ranking institutes and universities. We aspire to instill confidence in them to pursue research careers in higher studies, equip them with practical skills, and nurture a sense of belonging within the computing research community.

About

Who can apply
Bachelors' and Master's students with computer science background in their pre-final or final year are eligible to apply for this workshop. It will be held in both virtual and onsite formats.
Experience needed
Participation does not require any previous research experience, but it can be advantageous to have a background in programming language such as C/C++/Python/Java and to have successfully completed introductory coursework in computer science.
Research activities
During the workshop, participants will be teamed up with one or two faculty mentors, who will provide continuous guidance throughout the workshop and beyond. A broad spectrum of research endeavors involving a foundational understanding of the domain, familiarity with the current state of the art, collaboration with faculty in existing laboratory projects, and the initiation of new independent research initiatives. After the onsite event concludes, participants will have the opportunity to showcase their work through poster presentations, engage in workshops, interact with mentors, explore research laboratory facilities, and participate in hands-on activities. Moreover, there is the potential for extending and publishing these projects as short or full papers.
Time commitment
The Mentorship Program spans approximately one semester, featuring two to three days virtual sessions, followed by four days taking place on the IIT Roorkee campus. During this event, 10-12 faculty members, each accompanied by their Teaching Assistant, will be actively involved in presenting and interacting with the students. We anticipate that research group meetings, mentor sessions, and project work will require a minimum commitment of 4 hours per week from the beginning to the end of the workshop.
Google Award
Selected participants will receive a google award up to 15,000 INR, that will cover the expenses of accomodation and fooding along with some additional goodies based on their performance.
